Research Topic: King Arthur, Arthurian Legends & Excalibur

Hail, Investigator! Your task is among the most vital of the entire quest. You must uncover the origins of the legend itself ‚Äî who King Arthur truly was, how the Arthurian myths were born, and the secrets behind the legendary sword Excalibur.

Search 2‚Äì3 of the websites listed below, answer the guiding research questions, and take careful notes on your handout. Your team is counting on your findings. The truth of Camelot begins with you.

Part One

King Arthur ‚Äî Fact & Legend

Who Was King Arthur?

  • 1.Who was King Arthur? Describe his origins and background in your own words.
  • 2.Who were Arthur's parents? What were the unusual circumstances of his birth and early childhood?
  • 3.Who was Uther Pendragon, and what was his relationship to Arthur?
  • 4.How did Arthur become king? What was the significance of the sword in the stone?
  • 5.Who was Merlin, and what role did he play in Arthur's life and rise to power?

Camelot & Arthur's Reign

  • 6.What was Camelot? Describe what it looked like and where it was believed to be located.
  • 7.Who was Queen Guinevere? What do we know about her relationship with Arthur?
  • 8.Who was Mordred? How was he related to Arthur, and what role did he play in the downfall of Camelot?
  • 9.How did Arthur's reign come to an end? Describe his fate.
  • 10.What is Avalon? How is it connected to the legend of King Arthur?

Excalibur ‚Äî The Legendary Sword

  • 11.What was Excalibur? Describe the sword and its legendary powers.
  • 12.Where did Excalibur come from? Who gave it to Arthur, and under what circumstances?
  • 13.What was the significance of Excalibur's scabbard? What did it protect Arthur from?
  • 14.What happened to Excalibur after Arthur's final battle? Who returned it, and to whom?
  • 15.Is the "sword in the stone" the same as Excalibur, or are they two different swords? Explain.

Legacy & Historical Truth

  • 16.Was King Arthur a real historical figure? What evidence exists that he may (or may not) have actually lived?
  • 17.Name at least two famous books, poems, or written works based on the Arthurian legend.
  • 18.Why do you think the legend of King Arthur has endured for so many centuries? What makes it timeless?
