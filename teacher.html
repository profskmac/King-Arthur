The Teacher

Research Topic: Life During the Medieval Ages

Welcome, wise Teacher! Your role is uniquely vital ‚Äî you are the keeper of historical context. While your teammates investigate legends and myths, you will bring the real world of the Middle Ages to life. What was it truly like to live in medieval times? How did people eat, work, worship, and survive?

Research 2‚Äì3 of the websites listed below, answer the guiding questions, and document your findings on your note-taking handout. Without your knowledge of medieval life, the legend of King Arthur is only half the story!

Part One

Medieval Society ‚Äî Structure & Order

The Feudal System

  • 1.What was the feudal system? Describe how medieval society was organized from the highest ranks to the lowest.
  • 2.What were the roles and responsibilities of a king in medieval society?
  • 3.Who were the nobles and lords? What power did they hold, and what were their obligations to the king?
  • 4.Who were the serfs or peasants? Describe their daily life, responsibilities, and limitations.
  • 5.What role did the Catholic Church play in medieval life? How much power did it have over people and governments?

Castles & Daily Life

  • 6.What was a castle? Describe its major parts and explain why castles were built the way they were.
  • 7.What was a great hall? How was it used, and who gathered there?
  • 8.Describe a typical day in the life of a medieval noblewoman or lord.
  • 9.What did medieval people eat? How was food prepared and preserved? Did it vary by social class?
  • 10.What did people wear in the Middle Ages? How did clothing reflect social rank?
  • 11.What forms of entertainment and recreation existed in the Middle Ages? Describe at least three.

Knights & Warfare

  • 12.How did a young man become a knight in the Middle Ages? Describe the steps from page to squire to knight.
  • 13.What weapons and armor did medieval knights use? Describe at least four types.
  • 14.What was a tournament? What was its purpose, and what events typically took place?
  • 15.What was a siege? How did medieval armies attempt to capture castles?

Religion, Education & the Arts

  • 16.How important was religion in medieval daily life? Describe how the Church influenced people's routines and beliefs.
  • 17.Who received an education in the Middle Ages, and where did they receive it? What subjects were taught?
  • 18.What were illuminated manuscripts? Who created them, and why were they important?
  • 19.What was the Black Death, and how did it affect life in the Middle Ages?
  • 20.How did the Middle Ages eventually come to an end? What major events or changes marked the beginning of a new era?
